# Data licence and source-text rights

## Licence scope

The [MIT License](LICENSE) applies only to software.

[CC BY 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/) applies only to
project-authored classifications, summaries, crosswalks, rationales, schemas,
editorial metadata, and records individually identified as project-authored
reference translations. At this snapshot, that last category includes the 81
current China Cybersecurity Law references; current references for LGPD
Articles 5, 55-A, and 55-C; 36 APPI references (13 changed main Articles, two
changed appended tables, and 21 supplementary-provision blocks); 79
Brazil PL 2338 references; 76 Indonesia PDP Law references; 11 Taiwan
Executive Yuan generative-AI guidance references; and 144 Vietnam references
covering Law 91/2025, Decree 356/2025 and historical Decree 13/2023. It does
not apply to verbatim official source text, government or official
translations, or embedded third-party material.

When reusing project-authored data, attribute **Compliance Compass
contributors**, identify the dataset version or commit, link to this
repository, and indicate whether changes were made.

Official source text included in this repository is governed by the
source-specific rights basis documented in its corpus manifest. Inclusion does
not place that text under CC BY 4.0 and does not make this repository an
official version of any legal instrument.

## Source-text rights

- The ANPD 2024 LGPD English reference is retained under Creative Commons
  Attribution-NoDerivs 3.0 Unported. Its PDF line wrapping is reflowed without
  substantive alteration. The three Articles changed by Law No. 15,352/2026
  use separately identified project-authored current references under CC BY
  4.0 and preserve the superseded ANPD wording as historical metadata.
- Japanese Law Translation Database English reference text is retained under
  the Government of Japan Public Data License (Version 1.0), with attribution
  and the source's no-legal-effect warning. For APPI, 172 units retain that
  reference after a normalized Japanese-text comparison verified that their
  wording is unchanged in the 2026 consolidation. The 15 changed main-Article
  and table units, and the 21 supplementary-provision blocks, are separately
  identified project translations under CC BY 4.0.
- Korean Law Information Center English references are retained under the
  Ministry of Government Legislation's Law Open Data reuse policy. Their
  no-official-effect notices and temporal alignment metadata remain attached;
  KLRI commercial text is not used.
- The NPC and PRC Ministry of Justice English legal publications are retained
  with attribution and reference-only authority labels under the corpus
  manifests' documented PRC Copyright Law Article 5 basis. The promulgated
  Chinese text controls.
- The CASI/Air University English reference for China's Interim Generative AI
  Measures is a United States Government work subject to 17 U.S.C. § 105. It
  is labelled as a public-sector reference, not as an official Chinese
  translation.
- Taiwan government legal text and government English references are retained
  under the source records' documented Taiwan Copyright Act Article 9 basis;
  authority and commencement distinctions remain attached to each corpus. The
  final Executive Yuan generative-AI guidance English layer is instead a
  separately labelled project reference under CC BY 4.0.
- Colorado session-law text is reproduced as a United States government edict
  that is not copyrightable under the government-edicts doctrine. It is not
  relicensed as CC BY.
- Brazilian PL 2338/2023 text is reproduced as the text of an official act
  excluded from copyright protection by Lei nº 9.610/1998, Article 8(IV). The
  signed or stamped official PDF layout is linked, not redistributed. Its
  complete English layer is a separately labelled project reference under CC
  BY 4.0 and does not imply that the proposal is law.
- Indonesia PDP Law and the three Vietnam corpora retain their controlling
  source-language government texts and lifecycle records. Their complete
  English layers are separately labelled, machine-assisted project references
  under CC BY 4.0; commercial or subscription translations were used only for
  terminology cross-checking and were not copied.
- NIST AI RMF 1.0 is a NIST Technical Series publication. NIST employee works
  are not copyrightable in the United States under 17 U.S.C. § 105; NIST also
  publishes worldwide reprint and derivative-work terms. Cite the original
  publication and state: “Republished courtesy of the National Institute of
  Standards and Technology.”
- Embedded ISO, OECD, or other third-party quotations, definitions, figures,
  and materials are excluded from the project licence and from any public-domain
  representation. Affected NIST nodes are identified in the corpus manifest.
- The proposed Canadian AIDA text is not redistributed because permission for
  republication of the unenacted Bill C-27 text has not been established. The
  repository provides official-source links, hashes, structural metadata,
  lifecycle findings, and project-authored summaries only.

## Materials outside the project licence

No project licence claims ownership of or grants additional rights in:

- official legislation, regulations, government guidance, translations, or
  public records beyond the source-specific basis documented for that corpus;
- third-party commentary, standards, figures, definitions, or quotations;
- commercial standards, paywalled publications, or proprietary databases;
- names, marks, seals, flags, or logos of governments and standards bodies.

ISO/IEC 42001 and IEEE *Ethically Aligned Design* are represented only by
project-authored metadata and summaries plus official access links. Consult the
corpus manifest and original publisher before redistributing any source text.
