# Code of conduct

We want this project to support careful disagreement across professions, languages, and legal traditions.

Participants must be respectful, evidence-focused, and open to correction. Harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory conduct, deliberate misrepresentation of sources, and repeated bad-faith disruption are not acceptable.

When reviewing a disputed mapping:

- critique the evidence or reasoning, not the contributor;
- distinguish translation questions from substantive legal questions;
- explain jurisdiction-specific context rather than assuming it;
- disclose relevant conflicts of interest when practical;
- give maintainers time to verify primary sources.

Project maintainers may edit, hide, reject, or remove contributions and participation that violate these expectations. Until a dedicated moderation address is published, report conduct concerns privately to a repository maintainer.
